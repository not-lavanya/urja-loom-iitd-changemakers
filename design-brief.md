# Design Brief

## Context

Handloom weavers often use repeated foot-pedal motion while operating looms. That motion is usually wasted as mechanical energy. Urja Loom explores whether a small part of that motion can be captured to support basic workspace lighting.

## Target Users

- Handloom weavers
- Tailors using foot-pedal machines
- Small workshop operators in areas with unreliable electricity

## Problem Statement

Provide an alternate source of clean, embedded energy for lighting in handloom or tailoring workspaces that experience erratic power supply.

## Constraints

- Users may be unfamiliar with new technology.
- Perceived cost must remain low.
- Maintenance must be simple.
- The device should not interrupt the user's natural work rhythm.
- Output must be visible enough to build trust.
- Hardware must tolerate repeated pressure, dust, and workshop conditions.

## Design Choice

The team considered alternate sources including piezoelectricity, flywheels, thermoelectric generation, and magnetohydrodynamic generation. Piezoelectric tiles were chosen for the initial prototype because they could be integrated directly under repetitive foot pressure and were comparatively easy to prototype.

## Core Insight

The goal is not maximum theoretical energy generation. The goal is useful energy generation that respects real user behavior.
