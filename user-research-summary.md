# User Research Summary

This summary is based on aggregated project notes and survey outputs. Raw survey responses are intentionally not included because they may contain personal information.

## Findings

- Respondents were interested in footstep or foot-motion powered clean energy when the impact was visible.
- Visual feedback, such as lights turning on or an output display, increased willingness to engage.
- Suggested installation contexts included educational campuses, metro stations, markets, and workspaces.
- Main concerns included cost, maintenance, vandalism risk, durability, and uncertainty about real output.
- Recycled materials and real-time energy output displays were suggested as improvements.

## Design Implications

- The system should clearly show generated energy.
- The installation should be rugged and simple.
- The value proposition should be immediate: safer lighting, reduced generator dependence, or backup lighting.
- The system should avoid demanding behavioral changes from workers.

## Research Direction

The next research step is not only electrical optimization. It is field validation:

- How much pressure does a weaver naturally apply?
- How consistent is that pressure over a work session?
- What lighting level is actually useful?
- How much maintenance is acceptable?
- What price point is viable for NGOs, cooperatives, or local workshops?
